\section{Overview}

BEM-CUDA solves the electromagnetic scattering problem for dielectric particles
using the Boundary Element Method with PMCHWT formulation. The $2N\times 2N$ system:
\[
\begin{pmatrix}
\eta_e L_{\text{ext}} + \eta_i L_{\text{int}} & -(K_{\text{ext}}+K_{\text{int}})\\
K_{\text{ext}}+K_{\text{int}} & \dfrac{L_{\text{ext}}}{\eta_e}+\dfrac{L_{\text{int}}}{\eta_i}
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}J\\M\end{pmatrix}
=
\begin{pmatrix}b_J\\b_M\end{pmatrix}
\]
where $L$, $K$ are single-layer and double-layer BEM operators,
$\eta_e=1$, $\eta_i=1/|m|$, and $m$ is the complex refractive index.

\subsection{GMRES Parameters (Iterative Solvers)}

\begin{longtable}{lllp{7cm}}
\toprule
Flag & Type & Default & Description\\
\midrule
\texttt{--gmres-tol F} & float & $10^{-4}$ & Relative convergence tolerance\\
\texttt{--gmres-restart N} & int & 100 & Restart parameter $m$ (Krylov subspace size)\\
\bottomrule
\end{longtable}

GMRES memory: $m \times 2N \times 16$ bytes. Example: $m=100$, $N=9216$ $\to$ 60\,MB.

\textbf{Important:} GMRES tolerance should not be tighter than FMM accuracy.
Without preconditioner, GMRES may fail to converge at ref $\ge 4$.

\[
\delta = \frac{0.69\lambda}{3R\,D_{\max}}, \quad
N_\alpha = \lceil 360^\circ/\delta \rceil, \quad
N_\beta = \lceil 180^\circ/\delta \rceil
\]

\section{Working with OBJ Meshes}

\subsection{Preparing the Mesh}

The OBJ file must contain a \textbf{closed, manifold, triangulated} surface:
\begin{itemize}
  \item Triangulated faces only (no quads)
  \item Closed surface (no holes)
  \item Manifold edges (each edge shared by exactly 2 triangles)
  \item Consistent face orientation (normals outward)
\end{itemize}

Repair tools: MeshLab (Filters $\to$ Cleaning), Blender (3D Print Toolkit).

\subsection{Mesh Resolution}

The solver prints diagnostics:
\begin{lstlisting}
Resolution: h_avg=0.1057, lambda_ext/h=11.9, lambda_int/h=9.1 [OK]
\end{lstlisting}

\begin{tabular}{lll}
\toprule
$\lambda_{\text{int}}/h$ & Status & Action\\
\midrule
$\ge 10$ & OK & Good resolution\\
$6$--$10$ & Marginal & 5--10\% error possible\\
$< 6$ & WARNING & Use \texttt{--subdiv} or reduce $ka$\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\subsection{Scaling}

The mesh is automatically scaled so $D_{\max}$ matches the size parameter $ka$. No manual scaling is needed.

\subsection{Subdivision}

\texttt{--subdiv N} performs $N$ rounds of flat subdivision: each triangle $\to$ 4 triangles.

Example: 1200-triangle OBJ $+$ \texttt{--subdiv 2} $=$ 19\,200 triangles $\approx$ 28\,800 RWG.

\subsection{Example: Arbitrary Object}

\begin{lstlisting}
# Quick test (1 orientation)
bin/bem_cuda --ka 5 --ri 1.3116 0 --obj model.obj --subdiv 1 \
  --solver auto --single --out test.json

# Full orientation averaging
bin/bem_cuda --ka 5 --ri 1.3116 0 --obj model.obj --subdiv 1 \
  --solver auto --orient-auto --out result.json
\end{lstlisting}

\section{Solvers}

\subsection{Dense LU (\texttt{--solver dense})}
Direct LU factorization via cuSOLVER. $O(N^3)$ time, $O(N^2)$ memory.
Practical for $N \le 8000$ (ref $\le 4$). No iterative convergence issues.

\subsection{FMM+GMRES (\texttt{--solver fmm})}
Plane-wave MLFMA with GMRES iterative solver.
Matrix-free $O(N\log N)$ matvec. GPU-accelerated P2P, P2M, M2L, L2P kernels.

\paragraph{P2P near-field.}
Leaf-to-leaf neighbor structure ($\sim$1\,MB) instead of point-to-point CSR ($\sim$6\,GB).
Each target looks up its leaf, iterates over neighboring leaves, and evaluates $G(\mathbf{r}_i, \mathbf{r}_j)$ for all sources in those leaves.

\paragraph{Two-stream pipeline.}
P2P and FMM tree traversal run concurrently on separate CUDA streams.

\paragraph{Shared tree.}
For PMCHWT (two wavenumbers $k_{\text{ext}}$, $k_{\text{int}}$), the octree, P2P structure,
and positions are shared. Only $k$-dependent arrays are duplicated.

\subsection{pFFT+GMRES (\texttt{--solver pfft})}
Precorrected FFT: 3D Cartesian grid with FFT-based far-field.

\subsection{Surface pFFT (\texttt{--solver spfft})}
2D FFT per flat face for hex prisms: FP32 C2C, CUDA streams, mixed-radix grids.

%% ===================================================================
\section{Preconditioners}

Right preconditioning: solve $ZM^{-1}(Mx) = b$.

\subsection{Block-Jacobi + RAS}
Spatial cell blocks with dense LU. Adaptive bisection for oversized blocks.
GPU-accelerated apply with warp-parallel triangular solve.

RAS overlap extends each block with neighboring RWGs:
\[
\text{overlap\_dist} = \text{overlap\_layers} \times 2\,\overline{h}_{\text{extent}}
\]

\paragraph{Benchmark} (hex $D/L=0.7$, $m=1.3116$, RTX 3080 Ti):

\begin{tabular}{cclcccc}
\toprule
ref & $ka$ & Precond & Blocks & Build\,(s) & Matvecs & Total\,(s)\\
\midrule
3 & 10 & none & -- & -- & 451 & 85\\
3 & 10 & blockj & 4 & 1.4 & 392 & 115\\
4 & 5 & blockj+RAS(1) & 73 & 31 & 22 & 50\\
\textbf{4} & \textbf{16} & \textbf{blockj+RAS(1)} & \textbf{12} & \textbf{154} & \textbf{14} & \textbf{184}\\
4 & 20 & blockj+RAS(1) & 4 & 2432 & 13 & 2476\\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}

\medskip
12-block RAS at $ka=16$: \textbf{51$\times$ fewer iterations, 13$\times$ faster} vs baseline.

\section{Examples}

\begin{lstlisting}
# 1. Dense LU, sphere, ka=5, single orientation
bin/bem_cuda --ka 5 --ref 3 --ri 1.3116 0 --single

# 2. FMM, hex D/L=0.7, Block-Jacobi+RAS, 64 orientations
bin/bem_cuda --solver fmm --ka 10 --ref 4 --shape hex --ar 1.4286 \
  --prec blockj --prec-overlap 1 --gmres-restart 200 \
  --orient 8 8 1 --out hex_ka10.json

# 3. Auto solver, auto orientations, hex
bin/bem_cuda --solver auto --ka 15 --ref 4 --shape hex --ar 0.7 \
  --orient-auto --out hex_ka15.json

# 4. OBJ mesh with subdivision
bin/bem_cuda --ka 5 --ri 1.5 0.01 --obj statue.obj --subdiv 1 \
  --solver auto --orient-auto --out statue_ka5.json

# 5. Cluster: split 805 orientations across 4 nodes
bin/bem_cuda --solver auto --ka 15 --ref 4 --shape hex --ar 0.7 \
  --orient 35 23 1 --orient-range 0 202 --out node0.json
# ... --orient-range 202 404, 404 606, 606 805

# 6. High-ka + RAS
bin/bem_cuda --solver spfft --shape hex --ar 0.7 --ka 20 --ref 4 \
  --prec blockj --prec-r 2.0 --prec-bs 1000 --prec-overlap 1 \
  --gmres-restart 200 --gmres-tol 1e-4 --orient 45 31 1 \
  --out hex_ka20_r4.json
\end{lstlisting}

\section{Build}

\begin{lstlisting}
make -j$(nproc)
\end{lstlisting}

For CUDA 12.8+ with gcc~15 (too new for nvcc):
\begin{lstlisting}
make -j$(nproc) NVFLAGS="-arch=sm_86 -O3 --use_fast_math \
  -ccbin g++-13 -Xcompiler '-O2 -Wall -std=c++11 -fopenmp' -std=c++11"
\end{lstlisting}

Set GPU architecture in \texttt{Makefile} (default: \texttt{sm\_86}).
